What is Vagrant?

Vagrant is a tool for building complete development environments, sandboxed in a virtual machine. Vagrant lowers development environment setup time, increases development production parity, and brings the idea of disposable compute resources down to the desktop.

Why Vagrant?

  • Installing/Configuring Development Environments can be a tedious task.
  • All installations are manual. Installing some of the software is not at all trivial, especially on Mac OS X/*NIX and Windows, the primary development platforms.
  • Configuration is even more difficult than installation. Developers are unlikely to know how to configure it to best resemble production. Misconfigured software can lead to functionality that appears to work in development, but doesnâ€™t work the same in production.
  • There is a stark difference between development and production environments. This leads to a lot of "works on my machine" bugs (i.e. bugs that don't appear on a developer's machine, but appear in production)
  • Managing multiple projects on a single development environment can get quite messy and leave the system in a brittle state. Some projects may require different servicesbackend that the others don't need.
  • Syncing development environments with other team members becomes a hassle.
  • New hires will have to spend again the same amount of time setting up complex development environment
  • Limited choices on using operating systems of your choice on your local desktop.
